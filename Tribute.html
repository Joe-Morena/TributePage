Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela

The Long Walk to Freedom

Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born Rolihlahla at Mveso in Transkei, Eastern Cape, South Africa.
  • 1925 - Attends primary school near Qunu and recieves the name "Nelson" from a teacher.
  • 1937 - Attends Healdtown, the Wesleyan College at Fort Beauford.
  • 1939 - Enrols at the University College of Fort Hare, in Alice and get expelled year later.
  • 1941 - Escapes arranged marriage and become a mine security.
  • 1942 - Completes BA through the University of South Africa; Begins to attend African National Congress meetings informally.
  • 1943 - Graduates with BA from Fort Hare; Enrols for an LLB at Wits University.
  • 1944 - Co-founds the ANC Youth League.
  • 1951 - Elected President of the ANCYL.
  • 1952 - Defiance Campaign begins; Arrested and charged for violating the Suppression of Communism Act; Elected Transvaal ANC President; Opens law firm with Oliver-Tambo, the only black law firm in Johannesburg in the 1950s.
  • 1955 - Watches as the Congress of the People at Kliptown adopts the Freedom Charter.
  • 1960 - Sharpville Massacre; A State of Emergency is imposed and he is among thousands detained; The ANC is banned.
  • 1961 - Goes Underground; Umkhonto Wesizwe is formed.
  • 1962 - Leaves the country for military training and to garner support for the ANC; Returns to South Africa and sentenced for five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport.
  • 1963 - Sent to Robben Island.
  • 1990 - ANC is unbanned. Released from Prison; Elected Deputy President of the ANC.
  • 1993 - Awarded Nobel Peace Prize alongside F.W. De Klerk.
  • 1994 - Votes for the first time in his life; Elected by Parliment as first president of a democratic South Africa; Launches his autobiography Long Walk to Freedom.
  • 1999 - Steps down as the President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation.
  • 2013 - Passes away at home in Johannesburg at the age of 95.

"I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die"

-- Speech from the Dock quote by Nelson Mandela on 20 April 1964
